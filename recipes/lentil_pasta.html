Lentil Pasta

Ok, pasta lovers, you need this lentil pasta recipe in your winter rotation! It’s budget-friendly,
easy to make, and comforting in the way that only pasta with red sauce can be. Though this lentil
pasta recipe is full of Italian flavors (marinara! balsamic! Parmesan!), it’s not a take on any
specific Italian pasta dish. Rather, it’s something I love to cook when I want a hearty, flavorful
pasta dinner, but I don’t want to go to the store or spend hours in the kitchen. This lentil pasta
recipe comes together with basic pantry ingredients, and you can have it on the table in right
around 30 minutes. I think you’re going to love it!

ingredients

Here’s what you’ll need to make this lentil pasta recipe:

  • Lentils

    They pack this pasta with nutrients like fiber and
    plant-based protein. I like to use French green lentils
    because they retain a nice bite when cooked. Regular
    green or brown lentils will work too, but avoid red
    lentils, as they turn too soft.

  • Pasta

    Pappardelle is my noodle of choice. Its wide, chewy
    ribbons work well with the hearty lentil sauce. If you
    can’t find it, tagliatelle is a great sub. For a vegan
    option, use egg-free fettuccine or linguine. Gluten-free?
    Use your favorite alternative!

  • Onion, carrots, and garlic

    They create the flavorful base of the sauce, adding
    rich depth to the dish.

  • Cremini mushrooms

    They add a rich, savory complexity to the sauce,
    enhancing its umami depth.

  • Tamari and balsamic vinegar

    They bring out the deep umami flavors in the
    mushrooms, making the sauce even more robust.

  • Extra-virgin olive oil

    Used for sautéing the veggies, enhancing their
    flavors, and adding richness.

  • Marinara sauce

    Homemade or store-bought, it gives the dish its
    classic, rich tomato flavor. I like making mine with
    crushed tomatoes, dried oregano, garlic, and red
    pepper flakes.

  • Parmesan cheese

    For topping the pasta, adding nutty, salty richness.
    For a vegan option, use dairy-free Parmesan.

  • Fresh parsley

    Sprinkle it over the pasta for a fresh, vibrant
    finish that brightens up the dish.

  • Salt and pepper

    Essential for making all the flavors pop and
    bringing balance to the dish.

steps

  1. Use pre-made marinara. As much as I love to make my marinara sauce from scratch,
    doing so adds a whole 30 minutes to this recipe… NOT what I want on a busy weeknight.
    If I’m lucky, I’ll have some leftover marinara on hand in the freezer, but if I don’t,
    I use store-bought here. Rao’s is my favorite brand. It makes this recipe super flavorful
    and amazingly fast!
  2. Keep cooked lentils on hand in the freezer. They’ll come in handy not only for this
    lentil pasta recipe, but for grain bowls and salads as well! If you don’t have cooked
    lentils handy, start this recipe by cooking 1/2 cup dry lentils according to this
    instructions in this post.
  3. Start by cooking the pasta in a large pot of salted boiling water. When the pasta is
    al dente, drain it and toss it with a little olive oil to prevent sticking.
  4. Next, sauté the veggies. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add
    the onion and carrot and cook, stirring occasionally, until they soften, 5 to 8 minutes.
    Then, add the mushrooms, garlic, salt, and pepper, and cook until the mushrooms are
    tender, another 5 to 8 minutes.
  5. Add the remaining sauce ingredients and simmer. Stir in the balsamic and tamari,
    followed by the lentils and marinara. Cook, stirring, until the sauce is well combined
    and heated through, 2 minutes or so.
  6. Finally, mix in the pasta. Add it to the skillet and toss to coat in the sauce.
    Season to taste…
